About Me

My name is Mohammad Akariya, and I am from Kabul, Israel. I graduated from high school in 2018, and soon after I began working at Alrowad (2018–2019), an educational center where I helped organize school activities such as building robots and running scientific events inside classrooms. This role allowed me to work closely with students, improve my communication and teamwork skills, and gain early exposure to both technology and education.

In 2019, I began studying Electricity Engineering at Ben Gurion University, where I continued until 2022. During this time, I developed technical and analytical skills, but I eventually realized that my true passion was not in electrical engineering. This led me to reorient my academic path toward computer science and data-focused fields. In 2023, I started studying Computer Science at the University of Haifa, later transitioning into the Data Science program, where I am currently in my second year. This change has motivated me to pursue a career that combines both problem-solving and creativity, especially in Full Stack development and software engineering.

Alongside my academic studies, I worked different jobs to support my education and personal growth. Between 2023 and 2024, I worked at a gas station, where I learned the value of responsibility, time management, and perseverance. In 2025, I worked at the ice cream shop “Lagenda,” where I strengthened my interpersonal skills, teamwork abilities, and adaptability in a customer-oriented environment. These experiences, though not directly technical, taught me discipline, hard work, and communication skills that I now carry with me into my professional and academic life.

From a technical perspective, I have built a strong foundation in computer science. I have completed important courses such as Software Engineering, Probability & Statistics, and Data Structures. I am skilled in C, C++, Java, and Python, and I also have experience in web development (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript). In addition, I frequently use tools such as GitHub for version control, Visual Paradigm for UML modeling, VMware for system simulations, and Valgrind for debugging and memory management. These skills allow me to confidently work on diverse projects and adapt quickly to new challenges.

I have applied my knowledge in several academic and technical projects. One of the most notable is a network-based Flower Shop system, where I designed and implemented a detailed UML architecture across multiple layers. This project sharpened my skills in system design, teamwork, and software engineering principles. Another project was developing a Linux kernel module for encryption (Caesar and XOR ciphers), which gave me hands-on experience in low-level programming and operating systems. I also implemented a concurrent linked list in C using pthreads with hand-over-hand locking for thread safety, and I developed Java-based projects such as PrimeThreads and Producer-Consumer, which enhanced my understanding of multithreading and synchronization. These projects collectively strengthened my problem-solving, debugging, and system design skills.

On a personal level, I am a fast learner, motivated problem-solver, and strong team player. I enjoy coding as both a study and a hobby, experimenting with new languages and frameworks in my free time. Outside of technology, I enjoy playing basketball, which builds discipline and teamwork, and reading, which broadens my knowledge and perspective. I am also multilingual, fluent in Arabic, Hebrew, and English, which enables me to work effectively in diverse teams and multicultural environments.

Overall, my journey has been one of growth, persistence, and continuous learning. From my early experiences in education and engineering to my current focus on Data Science and software development, I have developed a wide range of skills and qualities that prepare me for the professional world. I am eager to contribute my technical abilities, adaptability, and dedication to meaningful projects in the field of software development, Full Stack engineering, and related areas.
